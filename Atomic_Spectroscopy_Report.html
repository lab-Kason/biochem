b) Temperature Profile in GF-AAS Determination

The temperature program in Graphite Furnace AAS (GF-AAS) consists of distinct stages:

======================================================================
GF-AAS TEMPERATURE PROGRAM STAGES
======================================================================

1. DRYING STAGE (50-150C):
    Purpose: Remove solvent from the sample
    Duration: 20-30 seconds
    Prevents spattering and ensures uniform sample distribution

2. PYROLYSIS/ASHING STAGE (350-1200C):
    Purpose: Remove matrix components and organic matter
    Temperature optimized to avoid analyte loss
    Reduces background interference

3. ATOMIZATION STAGE (2000-3000C):
    Purpose: Rapidly vaporize and atomize the analyte
    Maximum temperature reached quickly
    Measurement taken during this stage

4. CLEANING STAGE (>2500C):
    Purpose: Remove residual material from graphite tube
    Prepares furnace for next sample
    Extends tube lifetime
======================================================================

c) Advantages of GF-AAS over Flame AAS for Lead Determination in Sediment

When analyzing lead (Pb) in sediment samples, GF-AAS offers several significant advantages over Flame AAS:

========================================================================================================================
ADVANTAGES OF GF-AAS OVER FLAME AAS FOR LEAD DETERMINATION IN SEDIMENT
========================================================================================================================

                  Advantage                                      GF-AAS                               Flame AAS                  Significance for Pb in Sediment
   1. Detection Sensitivity  Ultra-high sensitivity (ppb to ppt levels)       Moderate sensitivity (ppm levels) Critical: Environmental Pb often at trace levels
  2. Sample Volume Required                       5-50 L (microliters)                    2-5 mL (milliliters)        Important: Limited sample after digestion
        3. Detection Limits                          ~0.1-1 g/L for Pb                      ~10-50 g/L for Pb               Essential: Meet EPA/WHO guidelines
     4. Matrix Interference Better control with temperature programming More chemical interferences from matrix       Important: Sediments have complex matrices
     5. Sample Introduction         Direct injection into graphite tube          Continuous aspiration required             Practical: Small samples, less waste
  6. Atomization Efficiency                   ~90-95% (enclosed system)                     ~5-10% (open flame)          Critical: Better precision and accuracy
       7. Cost per Analysis       Higher initial cost, lower per sample   Lower initial cost, higher per sample           Acceptable: Small samples justify cost
8. Environmental Compliance    Can detect trace Pb at regulatory levels     May miss low-level Pb contamination              Regulatory: Required for compliance

========================================================================================================================

 KEY REASONS FOR CHOOSING GF-AAS:

 Lead contamination in sediments is typically at trace levels (ppb)
 Sediment matrices are complex and may contain interfering elements
 Regulatory standards require detection at very low concentrations
 Small sample volumes are often available after digestion
 Higher sensitivity ensures compliance with environmental regulations
 Better precision for risk assessment and remediation decisions
